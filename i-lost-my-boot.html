I Lost My Boot

A song for Marie

Verse 1

I lost my boot, I lost my boot, I lost my boot and I fell on the ground. I looked in the closet, I looked under the bed, I looked in the bathtub but it wasn't there instead.

Chorus

Oh where, oh where did my little boot go? Oh where, oh where can it be? With my foot so cold and the floor so hard, Please bring my little boot back to me!

Verse 2

I lost my boot, I lost my boot, I lost my boot and I fell on the ground. I asked the dog and I asked the cat, They both just shrugged and sat.

Chorus

Oh where, oh where did my little boot go? Oh where, oh where can it be? With my foot so cold and the floor so hard, Please bring my little boot back to me!

Verse 3

I looked in the garden, I looked in the tree, I looked in the fishbowl (the fish looked at me). I checked in the fridge behind the orange juice, No boot — just a carrot that was coming loose.

Final Chorus

Oh where, oh where did my little boot go? Oh where, oh where can it be? (speaking, building suspense) Wait... I think I feel something... IT WAS ON MY FOOT THE WHOLE TIIIME!

Tag

I had my boot, I had my boot, I had my boot and I fell on the ground. (laughing)
Simple enough for a 3-year-old call-and-response. The fish line usually kills at that age. "It was on my foot the whole time" is the toddler twist — Marie will want to shout it.
